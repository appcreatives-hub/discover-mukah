The Heart of Melanau Culture

Often regarded as a paradise for food lovers, Mukah offers an array of exotic delicacies such as Umai and Sago. One of the most vibrant highlights of the year is the Kaul Festival, an annual celebration showcasing traditional boat races, rituals, and authentic local cuisine.

Ancient History

The Legend of Jerunei

The Jerunei Sungai Misan and Sungai Teloh sites preserve the history of Melanau burial poles. Carved from Belian wood, these poles once housed noble leaders and sacred offerings. Though some no longer stand, their stories of spiritual protection and the ipok ceremonies remain alive.

Culture & Fashion

Sapan Puloh & Traditional Attire

The Sapan Puloh Mini Museum preserves artifacts like Terendak hats and the Tall House. Discover the elegance of Melanau women's attire: black velvet blouses with kepih dada (chest ornaments), gold necklaces, and songket sarongs.

Exotic Delicacy

Ulat Sago (Sago Worms)

For the adventurous, Ulat Sago is a must-try. These are the larvae of the red palm weevil found in decaying sago trunks. Creamy, soft-bodied, and rich in protein and healthy fats, they are a traditional superfood.

Learn more about essential ingredients like sago, seafood, and tropical fruits that define Mukah's kitchen.
